# Customer Segmentation and Spending Behavior Analysis

## Overview
This project analyzes customer demographics and spending patterns to identify high-value customer segments and derive business insights. The analysis was performed using Python for data preparation and exploration, and Tableau for visual storytelling and dashboard development.

The goal of this case study is to demonstrate how structured customer data can be translated into actionable business recommendations through segmentation, behavioral analysis, and visualization.

---

## Business Objective
To understand customer spending behavior and identify patterns that can support:

- customer segmentation
- targeted marketing strategies
- improved retention planning
- data-driven business decision-making

---

## Dataset
**Dataset Used:** Mall Customers Dataset

The dataset includes customer-level attributes such as:
- CustomerID
- Genre
- Age
- Annual Income (k$)
- Spending Score

---

## Tools and Technologies
- **Python**
- **Pandas**
- **Tableau**

---

## Project Workflow

### 1. Data Loading and Exploration
- Loaded the customer dataset using Pandas
- Reviewed structure, columns, and summary statistics
- Validated available fields for analysis

### 2. Data Analysis
Performed exploratory analysis to study:
- spending behavior by customer group
- age-based spending patterns
- relationship between annual income and spending score
- high-value customer identification

### 3. Customer Segmentation
Created customer segments based on spending behavior:
- High Spender
- Medium Spender
- Low Spender

This segmentation helped simplify customer profiling and business interpretation.

### 4. Visualization
Built Tableau visuals and dashboard components to present:
- spending by segment
- income vs spending patterns
- age-related spending trends
- distribution of customer groups

---

## Key Insights

### 1. High-Value Customers Form a Small but Important Segment
A relatively small group of customers shows significantly higher spending behavior. These customers represent strong potential for retention and personalized engagement strategies.

### 2. Income Is Not a Strong Standalone Predictor of Spending
The analysis suggests that higher annual income does not always correspond to higher spending. This indicates that spending behavior is influenced by additional demographic or behavioral factors.

### 3. Younger Customers Show Higher Spending Tendencies
Customers in younger age groups tend to demonstrate higher spending scores compared to older groups, highlighting a valuable target audience for certain campaigns.

### 4. Segmentation Improves Strategic Clarity
Dividing customers into high, medium, and low spender categories makes it easier to interpret behavior patterns and align business actions accordingly.

### 5. Multi-Factor Analysis Is Essential
The weak relationship between income and spending reinforces the need to evaluate customer behavior through multiple variables rather than relying on one indicator alone.
